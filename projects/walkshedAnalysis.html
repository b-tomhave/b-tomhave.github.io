--- title: Walkshed Analysis and Summary subtitle: Using Tableau To Support Transit Planning Decision Making Process layout: "page" icon: order: hide: true ---

The number of people and jobs within a 5 or 10 minute walk of a proposed station are key criteria transit agencies use when determining where to locate future bus stations. By comparing the 5 and 10-minute walksheds, certain bus stations can be prioritized if they have high access while other stations may be passed over. To more easily draw these comparisons, I created an interactive Tableau Dashboard (below) that displays the total population, BIPOC population, and total number of jobs within a selected station's walkshed. The bar chart on the far right allows users to select a variable of their choosing to view the walkshed value for all stations along the corridor from north to south to allow for easy corridor-wide comparisons. To use the interactive dashboard, hover over (or select) a given station to view the selected walkshed information.

(Elements on the dashboards can appear compressed on smaller screens. For optimal viewing please view on a tablet or computer.)
