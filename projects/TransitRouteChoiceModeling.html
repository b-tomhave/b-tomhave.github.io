To provide faster and more reliable service, Metro Transit is implementing serveral arterial bus rapid transit (BRT) lines throughout the Greater Minneapolis--St. Paul region. While increased stop spacing and off board fare payment mechanisms contribute to this goal, additional bus priority treatments (or transit advantages) such as bus lanes, transit signal priority (tsp), and bus queue jumps also play a significant role.

The net benefit of bus priority treatments greatly depends on where these treatments are situated as traffic congestion and passenger loads are continuously flucuating both in time and space. Given that transit advantages cannot span the entire corridor, I created an interactive Tableau dashboard to answer the question: Where, and at what time of the day, will bus priority treatments provide the most benefit to transit passengers.

To allow for the greatest user flexibility, my dashboard presents a set of recommended bus priority locations (Part F in the dashboard below) in addition to an 8-step sequential process in which users can interact with and explore the data themselves in order to draw their own conclusions.

(Elements on the dashboards can appear compressed on smaller screens. For optimal viewing please view on a tablet or computer.)
